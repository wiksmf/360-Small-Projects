According to Wikipedia, the Pythagorean theorem states that the area of the square whose side is the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the areas of the squares on the other two sides.

This theorem can be written as an equation relating the lengths of the sides a, b and c, often called the Pythagorean equation: a2 + b2 = c2, where c represents the length of the hypotenuse and a and b the lengths of the triangle's other two sides.
