Find the: LCM GCD

According to Wikipedia, the least common multiple (lcm) of two or more integers is the smallest positive integer that is divisible by each of the integers.

Examples: lcm (4, 6)

  • Multiples of 4 are: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32,36, ...
  • Multiples of 6 are: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, ...
  • Common multiples of 4 and 6 are the numbers that are in both lists: 12, 24, 36, ...
  • Among this list, the smallest number is 12, hence the least common multiple is 12.

According to Wikipedia, the greatest common divisor (gcd) of two or more integers, which are not all zero, is the largest positive integer that divides each of the integers.

Examples: gcd (54, 24)

  • The divisors of 54 are: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18, 27, 54
  • The divisors of 24 are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
  • Common divisors of 54 and 34 are the numbers that are in both lists: 1, 2, 3, 6
  • Among this list, the greatest number is 6, hence the greatest common divisor is 6.
